TF-IDF Values Explanation

Understand the mathematics behind document ranking: from raw counts to weighted relevance scores for the query "hutan hujan".

1. Term Frequency (TF)

2. Inverse Doc Frequency (IDF)

3. Final TF-IDF Scores
1

Term Frequency (TF)

TF measures how frequently a term occurs in a document. The simplest form is a raw count of instances. In this example, we count occurrences of terms related to "Hutan Hujan" (Rainforest).

Formula

tf(t, d) = count(t, d)

Where t is the term and d is the document.

info

Note: More advanced systems may use logarithmic normalization like 1 + log(count) to dampen the effect of high frequency terms.

Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

IDF diminishes the weight of terms that occur very frequently in the document set and increases the weight of terms that occur rarely.

Formula

idf(t) = log
N df(t)

N: Total docs (3)
df(t): Docs containing term t

3

Final TF-IDF Scores

We multiply the TF scores by the IDF scores. High scores indicate that a word is both frequent in that specific document and relevant (not generic) across the corpus.

Formula

tfidf = tf × idf
